Training notes

A few short, practical notes — not filler content.

Assessment

Why "sore" isn't the goal

Soreness tells you a muscle was worked, not that it was worked well. Progress comes from consistent load over weeks, not how wrecked you feel the next day.

Recovery

Sleep is part of the program

Strength adaptations happen during recovery, not during the set. If sleep is inconsistent, that's a programming variable — tell your coach, don't just push through.

Mobility

Mobility work isn't a warm-up afterthought

Tight hips or shoulders usually explain a stalled lift better than the lift itself does. A short mobility block before training pays off more than it feels like it should.
